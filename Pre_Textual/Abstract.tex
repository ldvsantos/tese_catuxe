\begin{resumo}[Abstract]

\begin{otherlanguage*}{english}

Traditional Agricultural Knowledge and Systems (TAKS) held by \textit{quilombola} communities in the Semi-Arid Northeast~II Identity Territory (Bahia, Brazil) constitute intangible assets of high biocultural complexity, yet remain documentarily invisible to regulatory bodies and vulnerable to misappropriation. This thesis proposes and validates an integrated model for decoding, documenting, and safeguarding these assets, structured in five sequential and interdependent stages. The first stage conducted a PRISMA-ScR scoping review on the operational readiness of machine learning applied to traditional agricultural systems, yielding a pooled accuracy of 90.7\% (95\% CI 89.8--91.5\%) but critically low FAIR compliance (mean score 18.7/100), grounding the need for complementary approaches. The second stage performed a quantitative meta-analysis of biocultural vulnerability dimensions, identifying eight dimensions with differentiated effect sizes and moderation profiles varying according to geographic and institutional context. The third stage operationalized these dimensions through cross-cultural adaptation of the WOCAT-SLM questionnaire and structured elicitation of linguistic variables via the Delphi method, converting tacit knowledge into measurable criteria with a Content Validity Index above 0.85. The fourth stage integrated psychometric calibration via Item Response Theory (Masters' Partial Credit Model) with Mamdani-type fuzzy inference to construct the Biocultural Safeguard Index (BSI), which classifies knowledge into five urgency levels (Consolidated, Monitoring, Attention, High, Critical) on a continuous 0--100 scale, with weights informed by the meta-analytic effect sizes. The fifth stage evaluates the probative efficacy of a computational normative audit architecture, comprising a Bayesian Network for structural compliance, NLP/SBERT for semantic verification, and permissioned distributed ledger technology for cryptographic anchoring, in the automated verification of dossier compliance against the requirements of IPHAN, INPI, CGen, and FAO-GIAHS. The integrated model incorporates data governance according to FAIR principles and produces auditable evidence to support cultural heritage safeguarding processes, collective intellectual property registration, and candidacy for international preservation programs.

\textbf{Keywords}: Intangible Asset Safeguarding; Fuzzy Logic; Item Response Theory; Machine Learning; Traditional Agricultural Knowledge; \textit{Quilombola} Communities; Computational Audit.
 \end{otherlanguage*}
\end{resumo}
